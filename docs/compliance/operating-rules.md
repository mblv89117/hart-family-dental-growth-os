# Operating Rules & Compliance

1. Do not make unauthorized changes to live systems.  
2. Back up or document current settings before major changes.  
3. Use least-privilege access.  
4. Never expose credentials.  
5. Never place passwords in source code.  
6. Never store patient information in unauthorized systems.  
7. Comply with HIPAA and applicable privacy, advertising, dental, telehealth, email, SMS, and consumer-protection requirements.  
8. Require proper clinical supervision for teeth-straightening treatment.  
9. Do not make medical diagnoses.  
10. Do not guarantee clinical outcomes.  
11. Do not make false scarcity claims.  
12. Do not invent testimonials, reviews, providers, prices, credentials, or treatment capabilities.  
13. Obtain approval before publishing pricing, discounts, financing terms, clinical claims, or patient media.  
14. Clearly separate marketing automation from clinical decision-making.  
15. Escalate compliance, privacy, security, or patient-safety concerns immediately.  
16. Optimize for booked/completed appointments, accepted treatment, collected cash revenue, and long-term reputation — not vanity metrics.
